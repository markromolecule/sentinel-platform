# Incident Logs Scoping & Access Control Model

## Overview

To ensure data privacy, confidentiality, and proper administrative scoping, access to exam proctoring incident logs is restricted based on user roles and institutional hierarchy (Institution -> Department -> Course -> Section/Classroom -> Exam). This model establishes clear boundaries for viewing and managing incident telemetry.

---

## Roles & Access Boundaries

The visibility of incident logs is dynamically determined by the user's role and the context to which the exam is assigned.

| Role | Visibility Scope | Scope Level | Access Behavior |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Superadmin** | Department-wide | Department | Can view incident logs for any exam assigned to a classroom or section within their designated **Department**. |
| **Admin** | Course-wide | Course | Can view incident logs for exams assigned to classrooms or sections under their designated **Course**. Strict isolation prevents viewing logs outside of their assigned course. |
| **Instructor** | Ownership / Assignment-bound | Exam | Can view incident logs only for exams that they own (created) or that are explicitly assigned to them. |

---

## Scoping Resolution Rules

### 1. Superadmin (Department Level)
* If an exam is assigned to a **Classroom** or **Section** that belongs to a **Department**:
  * Any Superadmin of that Department can view all incident logs generated by attempts on that exam.
  * Superadmins automatically inherit visibility of all logs within their department boundaries.

### 2. Admin (Course Level)
* If an exam is assigned to a **Classroom** or **Section** that belongs to a specific **Course**:
  * Admins assigned to that Course can view the corresponding incident logs.
  * Admins **cannot** view incident logs of exams assigned to classrooms/sections associated with other courses, even if those courses belong to the same department.

### 3. Instructor (Exam/Ownership Level)
* Instructors are strictly limited to their own boundary:
  * Can only fetch and view incident logs for exams they created.
  * Can only fetch and view incident logs for exams where they have been explicitly assigned as a proctor or instructor.

---

## Application Scope

These access rules must be enforced across all client applications and backend APIs:

1. **`sentinel-core` (Admin/Superadmin Portal)**
   * Enforce superadmin-department and admin-course scoping when displaying incident statistics and logs in the administrative dashboard.
2. **`sentinel-web` (Instructor Portal)**
   * Enforce instructor-specific exam assignment and ownership checks when accessing student incident feeds.
3. **`sentinel-support` (Support Portal)**
   * Scopes support views to their respective operational boundaries (e.g. cross-tenant visibility or specific institution scopes).

---

## System Configuration

* **Permission Management Page**: 
  * Incident log viewing permissions must be toggleable/configurable on the permission settings page.
  * Roles must be mapped explicitly to these query constraints (e.g., matching the `assessments:view-incidents` permission with appropriate filters).
